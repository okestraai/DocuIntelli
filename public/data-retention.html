Data Policy, Retention & Deletion

Last updated: February 19, 2026

1. Purpose & Scope

This Data Policy defines how DocuIntelli AI ("we", "our", "us") collects, uses, protects, retains, shares, and deletes personal and consumer data across our document management platform. It applies to all personal data, consumer financial data, uploaded documents, AI-generated content, usage records, and metadata processed by the DocuIntelli AI service.

This policy is designed to comply with applicable data privacy laws including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) where applicable to financial data, and other relevant regulations. It is reviewed at least annually and updated as needed.

2. Data We Collect

Account Information

When you create an account, we collect your email address and display name. If you sign in with Google OAuth, we receive your name, email address, and profile picture from Google's authentication service. Passwords are hashed using industry-standard algorithms and are never stored in plaintext.

Documents & Content

We store the documents you upload (PDFs, Word documents, images), including the file content, user-provided metadata (name, category, tags, expiration date), and AI-generated data (extracted text, text chunks, vector embeddings, summaries, auto-generated tags). Document content is processed by our AI systems solely to provide the features you use.

Financial Data

If you connect a financial account via Plaid or another third-party provider, we may receive account balances, transaction history, and account identifiers. We store only the data necessary to provide the connected feature. We do not store your bank credentials — authentication is handled entirely by the third-party provider through secure OAuth flows.

Payment Information

Payment processing is handled by Stripe (PCI-DSS Level 1 compliant). We never receive, store, or process your full credit card number, CVC, or billing address. We retain only a Stripe customer ID and subscription status for billing management.

Usage Data

We collect usage information such as features accessed, documents uploaded, AI questions asked, authentication events, and subscription activity. This data is used to enforce plan limits, monitor system health, and improve the Service.

3. How We Use Your Data

  • Provide the Service: Store and process your documents, power AI-driven chat, analysis, and search, generate tags and embeddings, track document expirations, and deliver financial insights from connected accounts.
  • Account management: Authenticate your identity, manage your subscription tier, process payments, and enforce plan limits (document count, AI question quota, device limits).
  • Communications: Send transactional emails including document processing notifications, expiration alerts, weekly audit summaries, and account security alerts. You can manage notification preferences in Account Settings.
  • Security & fraud prevention: Detect and prevent abuse through rate limiting, device tracking, and authentication monitoring.
  • Service improvement: Analyze aggregate, anonymized usage patterns to improve features, performance, and user experience.

We do not: Use your document content or financial data for advertising, profiling, AI model training, or sell it to third parties. Your data is used solely to provide the features you have consented to.

4. Data Protection Measures

  • Encryption at rest: All data stored in our database is encrypted using AES-256 encryption. File storage is encrypted at the infrastructure level.
  • Encryption in transit: All data is transmitted over TLS 1.2 or TLS 1.3 exclusively. Older protocols are disabled. Forward-secrecy cipher suites are enforced.
  • Row-Level Security: Database-level security policies enforce data isolation — users can only access their own documents and records, even if application code were compromised.
  • Access-controlled file storage: Document files are accessed via time-limited, cryptographically signed URLs that expire after use. No public file URLs exist.
  • Token security: Third-party access tokens are stored encrypted at rest on the server and are never transmitted to or accessible from the frontend client.
  • AI processing isolation: Document content sent to our AI infrastructure travels over encrypted connections with authenticated access controls. The AI system does not retain document content beyond the duration of the processing request.
  • Network isolation: Production services are accessible only through a secure tunnel. Internal services (database, cache) are not exposed to the public internet.
  • Rate limiting: Per-IP rate limits are enforced on all API endpoints to prevent abuse and data scraping.

6. Financial Data Handling

DocuIntelli AI takes special care with consumer financial data obtained through third-party integrations (e.g., Plaid):

  • Explicit consent: Financial accounts are connected only through a user-initiated flow (Plaid Link). The consumer must authenticate with their financial institution and explicitly authorize access. We never pre-connect accounts or access financial data without direct consumer action.
  • Access tokens: Third-party access tokens are stored encrypted at rest on the server. They are never transmitted to the frontend client, logged, or included in error reports.
  • Data minimization: We retrieve and store only the data necessary to provide the requested feature (e.g., account balances, recent transactions). Raw API responses are not persisted beyond processing.
  • No secondary use: Financial data is never used for advertising, credit decisions, profiling, analytics beyond the requested feature, or sold to any third party.
  • No data aggregation: We do not aggregate consumer financial data across users for any purpose.
  • Revocation: Users may disconnect financial accounts at any time from Account Settings. Disconnecting immediately revokes the access token with the provider and permanently deletes all associated financial data from our systems.
  • Breach notification: In the event of unauthorized access to financial data, affected consumers will be notified within 72 hours in compliance with applicable regulations.

7. Consent & Consumer Choice

DocuIntelli AI obtains meaningful consumer consent at each stage of data processing:

  • Account creation: Users agree to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy during registration.
  • Document upload: Each document upload is a deliberate user action. We process documents only as instructed (AI analysis, embedding generation, tag generation).
  • Financial data access: Financial account connections are initiated explicitly by the consumer through Plaid Link's consent interface, which discloses what data will be accessed and how.
  • Email communications: Users can enable or disable specific notification categories (document reminders, security alerts, general notifications) from Account Settings at any time.
  • Withdrawal of consent: Users may delete documents, disconnect financial accounts, disable notifications, or delete their entire account at any time without penalty.

9. Automated Retention Enforcement

Retention limits are enforced automatically through scheduled database tasks, not manual processes:

  • Daily cleanup: Usage logs older than 30 days and notification logs older than 90 days are automatically purged.
  • Periodic cleanup: Limit violation records (180 days), review events (365 days), and orphaned document chunks (weekly) are purged on schedule.
  • Real-time deletion: When a user deletes a document, all associated data (file in storage, database records, text chunks, vector embeddings, tags, AI-generated metadata) is removed immediately in a single database transaction.
  • Financial data cleanup: When a user disconnects a financial account, the access token is revoked with the provider and all associated financial data is deleted immediately.

10. Document Deletion

Users may delete any individual document at any time from the Document Vault. Deletion is immediate and permanent:

  • The document file is permanently removed from file storage.
  • The database record is deleted, including all metadata (name, category, tags, expiration date).
  • All text chunks and vector embeddings derived from the document are cascade-deleted.
  • AI-generated tags and summaries are removed.
  • The document is excluded from all future search results, chat responses, and analytics.

Deleted documents cannot be recovered. This is by design to ensure complete, irreversible data removal.

11. Account Deletion Process

Users may request full account deletion at any time from their Account Settings. The process is as follows:

  1. User initiates deletion by typing the confirmation keyword "DELETE" in the Account Settings page.
  2. All documents owned by the user are permanently deleted, including files, chunks, embeddings, and metadata.
  3. All connected financial accounts are disconnected and access tokens are revoked with providers.
  4. All financial data associated with the user is permanently deleted.
  5. Subscription records and billing references are deactivated. Active Stripe subscriptions are cancelled.
  6. A confirmation email is sent to the user's registered email address.
  7. The user session is terminated immediately.
  8. Any residual account data (profile, preferences, usage history) is permanently purged within 30 days.

Account deletion can also be requested by emailing privacy@docuintelli.com. Requests submitted by email are processed within 30 days.

12. Consumer Rights

In accordance with GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable regulations, consumers have the following rights:

  • Right to know / access: You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. You can download your uploaded documents at any time from the Document Vault.
  • Right to correction: You may update your profile information (name, email preferences) from Account Settings at any time.
  • Right to deletion: You may delete individual documents, disconnect financial accounts, or request full account deletion (see Sections 10 and 11).
  • Right to data portability: You may export your documents in their original uploaded format at any time.
  • Right to restrict processing: You may contact us to request restrictions on specific data processing activities.
  • Right to object: You may opt out of non-essential data processing and email communications from Account Settings.
  • Right to non-discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your data rights.
  • Right to revoke consent: You may disconnect financial accounts, disable notifications, or delete your account at any time. Revoking consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing prior to revocation.

To exercise any of these rights, use the self-service controls in Account Settings or contact privacy@docuintelli.com. We respond to all verified requests within 30 days.

13. Children's Privacy

DocuIntelli AI is not intended for use by children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at privacy@docuintelli.com and we will promptly delete that information.

14. Cookies & Tracking

DocuIntelli AI uses minimal cookies and local storage, limited to what is necessary to operate the Service:

  • Authentication tokens: Session tokens stored in local storage to maintain your signed-in state.
  • User preferences: Theme preferences, notification settings, and UI state stored locally on your device.
  • Device identification: A device fingerprint is stored locally to enforce per-plan device limits on paid plans.

We do not use third-party tracking cookies, advertising pixels, or analytics trackers that share data with external advertising networks.

15. International Data Transfers

Our infrastructure is hosted in the United States. If you access the Service from outside the United States, your data will be transferred to and processed in the United States. Our infrastructure providers maintain appropriate safeguards for international data transfers, including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) where required by GDPR.

16. Data Breach Response

In the event of a data breach involving personal or financial data:

  • We will notify affected users within 72 hours of confirming the breach, in compliance with GDPR and applicable state-level breach notification laws.
  • The notification will describe the nature of the breach, the data affected, steps we have taken, and recommended actions for affected users.
  • Where required, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority (e.g., data protection authority under GDPR).
  • If financial data is involved, we will also notify the relevant financial data provider (e.g., Plaid) and cooperate with their incident response procedures.

Material changes are communicated to users via email or in-app notification. Updates are published on this page with a revised "Last updated" date.
